About Me

I’m Luca Recchia, a Bioengineering student at Northeastern University with a strong focus on biomechanics, medical devices, and applying engineering to real-world health challenges. Over the past several years, I’ve realized that the best way for me to learn is by building—taking an idea from a rough concept, turning it into something tangible, testing it, breaking it, and refining it until it actually works. That cycle of designing, analyzing, and improving is what drives me. My foundation in engineering has been deeply hands-on. Through coursework and project-based work in biomechanics and physiology, I’ve worked on structural analyses, implant design concepts, and mechanical modeling problems that require balancing theory with real-world constraints. I’m especially interested in how forces, materials, and biological systems interact—whether that’s analyzing internal loads, thinking about implant stiffness and stress distribution, or modeling how a system behaves under repeated loading. Beyond the classroom, I actively seek out opportunities that push me technically and professionally. I’ve pursued competitive co-ops and internships in biotech and pharma, preparing for roles that blend engineering, data, and translational research. I’m motivated by environments where I can contribute meaningfully, collaborate across disciplines, and continue developing both technical depth and leadership skills. Right now, I spend most of my time diving into advanced STEM coursework, preparing for co-op roles, and refining my technical toolkit—whether that’s through MATLAB modeling, mechanical analysis, or tackling complex problem sets that demand precision and persistence. I’m constantly looking for ways to expand what I can build and understand, especially in areas that sit at the intersection of engineering innovation and human health.

Air Brakes

As an Air Brakes Designer with NU Aerospace on the Launch Initiative rocket team, I design and 3D print PLA alignment jigs in SolidWorks to ensure precise attachment of the air brake body to the rocket airframe. In previous builds, many of the mounting holes were hand-drilled, which led to inconsistencies and longer assembly times, so I developed repeatable fixture designs that significantly improved alignment accuracy and reduced build time. I also designed a structural bulkhead for the rocket, working closely with the airframe team to integrate structural requirements into our design.

Self Tying Trash Can

As Designer and Manufacturer, I designed and built a smart self-tying trash can in a six-week development cycle alongside 3 team members, integrating an Arduino microcontroller with a load cell and distance sensor to automate waste detection and bag closure. I iterated through more than five different tying mechanisms to identify a solution that was both fast and reliable, ultimately delivering a fully functional and manufacturable prototype. The system was developed with accessibility in mind, specifically to assist individuals with limited mobility.

Intaking System 2023

As Systems Engineer for our 2023 FIRST Robotics season, I designed an intake mechanism capable of handling both inflatable cubes and rigid cones with a single system. The intake featured a polycarbonate inner roller tube with enlarged end caps, through which surgical tubing was strung to span the length of the roller; as the roller spun, the tubing expanded outward and conformed around the object, creating adaptive grip across varying shapes. I prototyped and tested numerous surgical tubing densities to balance durability and possession.
